Hello! I'm Ozgur Tuna Ozturk, a Mechanical Engineering Student with a Passion for the intersection of embedded and mechanical systems, coupled with a fascination for aerospace engineering.

My journey in engineering is all about finding the simplest solutions for all magnitudes of problems. In pursuit of this, I am dedicated to learn “how to make almost everything.” While learning, I created a variety of systems from a handheld 8-bit gaming device to a wearable biomedical device to enhance life, making it more advantageous. My childhood curiosity, inspired by family stories, led me to break apart, and reverse engineer things, fostering a love for learning and invention.

I hail from Istanbul, Turkey, and I'm currently pursuing a Bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering at Dartmouth College, alongside a Bachelor's in Physics and Computer Science at Wheaton College, MA.

I've been fortunate to travel widely, fostering a desire to experience diverse cultures, religions, and cuisines. My global perspective expanded even more while doing the International Baccalaureate at United World College (UWC), Changshu, China. These journeys instilled in me a deep appreciation for cultural understanding and global citizenship.

Believing in teamwork, I've seen diverse teams turn dreams into reality. I'm eager to blend my technical skills with a global perspective to make a meaningful impact on the world—together, we can change the world, one step at a time.

Driven by a persistent curiosity for "How to make almost everything," I've immersed myself in practical learning, expanding my knowledge through diverse projects.

In my latest projects, I engineered a Telehealth device connecting individuals in remote areas, the elderly, and those with disabilities to their families and healthcare providers. This user-friendly device, paired with my IOS app, enables accurate vital sign measurements and sharing. Notably, its single clickable body with display design with +360° rotation prioritizes accessibility, emphasizing the intersection of mechanical, electrical, and software engineering.

In one of my embedded electronic projects, I designed a handheld gaming device. This device uses an AVR microcontroller that can be reprogrammed using an SD card, optimizing memory usage, and allowing for the execution of expansive programs (games) directly from the card. This project enhanced my skills in datasheet examination and 8-bit assembly.

Currently, I'm constructing a DC Magnetron Sputtering deposition Device for my Senior Thesis, probing the correlation between medium pressure (argon) and deposition thickness. This endeavor aims to analyze plasma properties at increasing pressures. After completion, this project will also allow me to fabricate optical instruments and bring me one step closer to semiconductor fabrication.

Simultaneously, I'm developing a feature-rich handheld lamp with adjustable color and brightness controls, boasting mechanics like +360° rotation, magnetic attachment, and wireless charging.

Explore my engineering endeavors in the 'Projects' section on the navigation bar above.

As an Electrical Engineering and Embedded Systems instructor at Boston College's Makerspace Summer Camp, I led 20+ students in foundational electronics, PCB design, Arduino programming, and soldering. This showcased my pedagogical skills and ability to convey complex engineering concepts effectively.

At Dartmouth College, I excelled as a teaching assistant for courses like ENGS 21 and ENGS 22, also contributing as a Solidworks tutor at the Dartmouth Machine Shop. These roles allowed me to assist students in mastering engineering principles and refining practical skills.

Invited by the King of Bhutan, I collaborated on sharing Digital Fabrication methods with Bhutanese educators, covering diverse areas from 2D/3D design to PCB fabrication. This included lectures and hands-on workshops, culminating in educators creating final projects. We played a pivotal role in launching Bhutan's Super Fablab, ensuring machine functionality and facilitating local training. Effective teamwork enabled us to surpass goals, leaving enduring impacts as trained educators returned to disseminate knowledge, and students showcased their skills.

Since January 2022, I've served as the Head Makerspace Technician at Wheaton College, MA Innovation Spaces, overseeing technical operations and fostering an environment conducive to innovation and collaboration.

These experiences highlight my commitment to advancing engineering education and facilitating hands-on learning. Eager to apply my multifaceted skills and passion for engineering, I look forward to embracing new challenges and endeavors.
